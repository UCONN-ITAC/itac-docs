# Assessment Day Expectations
This page outlines expectations for students during an on-site assessment. It covers required safety practices, what to bring, team roles, and the types of activities that occur during a facility visit.

Students are expected to arrive prepared, follow all facility safety requirements, and actively participate in data collection and documentation throughout the assessment.

---

## Safety Expectations and What to Bring

Safety is the highest priority during any site visit. Manufacturing facilities contain moving equipment, high voltages, hot surfaces, and other hazards. Always follow the instructions of facility staff and adhere to posted safety requirements.

During the pre assessment meeting with the facility, the team will review required personal protective equipment (PPE) and the facility’s policy on photography. These requirements must be followed by all team members during the assessment.

!!! warning
    If you are unsure whether an area is safe to enter or whether PPE is required, ask the site lead before proceeding.

### Required Safety Practices

During the visit, all team members must:

- Follow all facility safety rules and posted signage
- Wear the PPE specified by the facility during the pre assessment meeting
- Remain within approved areas only
- Avoid interfering with production or employees
- Follow the facility’s photography policy

### What to Bring

Students should arrive prepared with the following items:

- **Business casual clothing** (ITAC branded clothing is recommended when available)
- **Closed toe shoes** (steel toe boots if required by the facility)
- **Safety glasses**
- **Notebook, pen, or tablet** for notes

!!! note
    Required PPE for the visit will be communicated to the team during the **pre assessment meeting**. Some facilities require additional equipment such as hard hats, hearing protection, or high visibility vests. These items are often provided on site, but students should bring their own PPE if they have it available.

## Purpose of the Assessment

The goal of an assessment visit is to understand how energy is used within the facility and identify opportunities for improvement.

During the visit, the team will:

- Observe facility operations
- Identify potential energy efficiency opportunities
- Document systems and equipment for the final report
- Collect the data required to support recommendations

The information collected during the visit forms the foundation for the recommendations developed after the assessment.

---

## What Happens During an Assessment

Most site visits follow a similar structure.

### 1. Introduction and Safety Briefing

The visit typically begins with a brief meeting between the assessment team and facility representatives. This may include:

- Introductions
- A safety briefing
- A high-level overview of the facility and its operations

### 2. Facility Walkthrough

The team will conduct a walkthrough of the manufacturing space to observe major energy systems and understand how equipment is used in practice.

Common systems evaluated include:

- Lighting systems
- HVAC equipment
- Compressed air systems
- Electric motors
- Manufacturing machinery
- Process equipment

During the walkthrough, students should take notes and photos (when permitted) to help document the equipment and operating conditions.

### 3. Data Collection

While on site, the team will gather the information needed to support later analysis. This may include:

- Equipment nameplate data
- Operating schedules
- System configurations
- Deployment of temporary sensors for data logging (such as current transducers, temperature sensors, or humidity sensors)
- Measurements using handheld instruments
- Observations of system operation
---

## Team Roles

Each assessment team has a designated **site lead** and divides responsibilities among members to ensure efficient data collection.

### Site Lead

The **site lead** serves as the primary point of contact with the facility and coordinates the team during the visit.

Responsibilities include:

- Leading discussions with facility staff
- Coordinating team members during the walkthrough
- Ensuring all key systems are evaluated
- Ensure all systems are documented

### Team Members

All team members are responsible for actively observing facility systems and documenting useful information during the walkthrough. While the site lead may coordinate tasks, students are expected to take initiative when they identify important information or potential opportunities.

Typical contributions include:

- Taking notes and documenting observations
- Recording equipment information
- Collecting measurements or deploying sensors
- Photographing systems when permitted by the facility
- Noting operational details that may be useful for later analysis

Effective assessments rely on active participation and communication within the team to ensure important details are not missed.

---

## Professional Conduct

Remember that you are representing ITAC and the university while on site.

Students are expected to:

- Be professional and respectful
- Ask thoughtful questions
- Listen carefully to facility staff
- Avoid disrupting production or employees
- Maintain confidentiality of any sensitive information

!!! warning
    Do not share photos, facility information, or assessment details outside of the project team unless explicitly approved.
